Xiushan Flower Lantern

Reviving a living tradition through design and technology.

Xiushan Flower Lantern is a traditional folk art from Chongqing, China — integrating dance, music, theater, craftsmanship, and folk belief. Despite being a national intangible cultural heritage, it struggles to reach the people who might care about it most.

The Problem

A national treasure that most people have never heard of.

Xiushan Flower Lantern carries centuries of Tujia, Miao, and Han cultural traditions. But its remote location, aging inheritors, and lack of digital presence have left it largely invisible to younger generations and the broader public. Existing preservation efforts are static and localized — they keep the form alive, but struggle to keep it relevant.

Research

What we found on the ground.

01

Literature Review

Reviewed academic sources on intangible cultural heritage, folk art preservation, and the history of Xiushan Flower Lantern.

02

Field Work

Visited Xiushan in person — museums, cultural sites, and live performances — to understand the tradition in its actual context.

03

Interviews

Spoke with local inheritors and practitioners to learn about the challenges they face and what the tradition means to them.

04

Survey

Collected responses from over 1,000 participants to measure public awareness and interest.

What We Built

Five ways to close the gap.

01

Digital Storytelling

A public account introducing the history, meaning, and artistry of Xiushan Flower Lantern — one post at a time.

02

Promotional Video

A mixed-reality film combining real footage and virtual environments to tell the story of the lantern's origin and craft.

03

Cultural Products

Tote bags, stickers, and decorative items designed around the visual language of the flower lantern — bringing tradition into everyday life.

04

Exhibitions & Workshops

Offline events in schools and public spaces where people could make lanterns and watch performances themselves.

05

AR Mini Program

A step-by-step AR guide that lets users learn and build a flower lantern through their phone — making craftsmanship interactive and accessible.
