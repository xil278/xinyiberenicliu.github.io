Designing for Attention

Understanding and supporting focus in digitally distracting environments.

An early-stage exploration into how digital behavior can be read as a quiet signal of cognitive state — and how design can respond with care, not control.

Status

Currently in exploration.

Research and concept sketches in progress. Full case study coming soon.

Premise

Screen time tells you how long.
Attention design asks how well.

Most digital wellbeing tools count minutes. This project is exploring a different lens — one that reads the shape of behavior, not just its duration.

What We're Exploring

Three things we keep coming back to.

01

Behavioral Signals

The small things you do on your phone, like switching apps, pausing, typing fast then slow, might say something about how your mind is doing.

02

Cognitive Translation

How do you turn those patterns into something meaningful? Something that feels like "you seem scattered right now" rather than raw data.

03

Gentle Feedback

Whatever the system surfaces, it shouldn't interrupt you. The goal is to support focus, not add noise or distraction.

What's Next
